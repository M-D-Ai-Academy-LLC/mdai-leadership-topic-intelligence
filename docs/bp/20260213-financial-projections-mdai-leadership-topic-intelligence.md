# Financial Projections: Leadership Topic Intelligence Platform

## 1. Executive Summary

This document provides a 3-year financial projection for `mdai-leadership-topic-intelligence`, a search analytics and topic intelligence platform for corporate leadership training demand discovery. The model assumes a productized B2B SaaS + services motion: subscription revenue for platform access, with optional onboarding and strategic advisory add-ons.

The base case projects break-even in Year 2 as recurring subscription revenue compounds and gross margin improves through automation, reusable workflows, and lower manual analyst effort per account. The strongest financial lever is annual contract value growth through mid-market and enterprise plans rather than one-off reporting work.

Recommendation: prioritize recurring subscription packaging, enforce disciplined customer acquisition spend, and treat services as a conversion and expansion mechanism, not the primary long-term revenue stream.

## 2. Background and Context

### Current Situation

- The project already includes core capabilities: keyword discovery, trend analysis, clustering, intent segmentation, content gap detection, and reporting.
- Current delivery is technically solid but not yet commercialized as a mature recurring revenue product.
- Market demand for AI-enabled market and content intelligence is increasing, but competition is high and buyers expect measurable ROI.

### Problem and Opportunity

Without a clear packaging and monetization plan, technical capability will not translate into predictable growth. The opportunity is to convert this platform into a repeatable subscription product for L&D teams, corporate training providers, and strategy/marketing teams focused on leadership content demand.

### Strategic Rationale

- Recurring subscription revenue is more predictable and valuable than ad hoc analysis engagements.
- Existing architecture supports API and report-driven use cases for multiple customer tiers.
- High-value insights can justify premium pricing when tied to measurable topic prioritization outcomes.

## 3. Analysis

### Revenue Model and Pricing Assumptions

#### Proposed Commercial Packaging

1. **Starter Plan** (SMB teams)
   - USD 699/month
   - Standard dashboards, monthly reports, limited API calls

2. **Growth Plan** (mid-market)
   - USD 1,999/month
   - Expanded limits, competitor analysis depth, priority support

3. **Enterprise Plan**
   - USD 4,999/month (average modeled ACV level)
   - Advanced integrations, custom models, governance and SLA

4. **Optional Services**
   - Onboarding package: USD 6,000 one-time
   - Quarterly strategy advisory: USD 7,500/quarter

### Customer and Conversion Assumptions (Base Case)

- Year 1 ending active customers: 30 total
  - 18 Starter, 9 Growth, 3 Enterprise
- Net revenue retention (NRR): 105% Year 1 -> 112% Year 3
- Gross logo churn: 12% Year 1 -> 9% Year 3
- Sales cycle assumptions:
  - Starter: 30-45 days
  - Growth: 45-75 days
  - Enterprise: 90-150 days

### Cost Structure

1. **COGS (direct platform delivery):** cloud, data provider/API costs, support
2. **R&D / Product:** engineering, model maintenance, platform enhancements
3. **Sales and Marketing:** demand gen, partnerships, SDR/AE workload, content ops
4. **G&A:** legal, finance, operations, tooling

### Unit Economics Targets

- Gross margin target:
  - Year 1: 72%
  - Year 2: 76%
  - Year 3: 80%
- CAC payback target:
  - < 16 months in Year 1
  - < 12 months by Year 3
- LTV/CAC target:
  - > 3.0 by end of Year 2

## 4. Strategy and Recommendations

### Proposed Financial Strategy

1. **Lead with subscriptions:** keep services tied to activation/expansion.
2. **Package for value:** price by decision impact and workflow depth, not raw data volume alone.
3. **Improve retention:** build onboarding and quarterly value reviews into standard account motion.
4. **Control burn:** phase hiring with booked ARR milestones.

### Key Initiatives

- Publish clear commercial packaging and contract terms.
- Implement usage and expansion telemetry (plan utilization, feature adoption).
- Establish enterprise proof-of-value templates for faster procurement.
- Standardize onboarding to reduce time-to-value and churn risk.

### Success Criteria

- ARR growth with controlled net burn.
- Improving gross margin and CAC payback.
- Strong NRR and reduced churn by segment.

### Responsibilities

- Product lead: roadmap and margin-enabling automation
- Revenue lead: pipeline, pricing discipline, forecast accuracy
- Customer success lead: onboarding, adoption, expansion

### Dependencies

- Reliable third-party data/API costs and uptime
- Clear ICP and pricing alignment

## 7. Risks and Mitigation

1. **Data/API cost volatility**
   - Mitigation: multi-source strategy, usage caps, contract renegotiation thresholds

2. **Long enterprise sales cycles**
   - Mitigation: pilot offers, proof-of-value milestones, procurement-ready security docs

3. **Churn from slow time-to-value**
   - Mitigation: guided onboarding, KPI baseline setup in first 30 days, QBR cadence

4. **Pricing pressure from alternatives**
   - Mitigation: stronger ROI narrative, segment-based packaging, premium enterprise features
